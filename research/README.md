## BCG — *Loyalty Programs Are Growing—So Are Customer Expectations* (Dec 9, 2024)

File: `BCG_Loyalty_Programs_2024.pdf` · Authors: Ed Crouch, Ben Eppler, Lauren Taylor, Christina
Mühlenbein, Elizabeth Hearne · Source: BCG loyalty program survey, May 2024 (second annual).

**Why it's here:** it's direct evidence for the problem AAA Insights addresses — companies increasingly
compete on *understanding what customers actually value and expect*, and feedback is how they learn it.

**Key takeaways relevant to the product:**
- As people join more loyalty programs, **loyalty and engagement decline** — competition for
  attention rises. The average US consumer now belongs to 15+ programs (up ~10% since 2022);
  the average European belongs to ~9.
- **Younger consumers switch brands and programs more readily** than older generations.
- Points and cash back are no longer enough — customers expect **personalized rewards, relevant
  partnerships, and exclusive experiences**. Expectations rise as awareness of program benefits grows.
- Traditional strongholds (hotels, airlines) are **less effective** at locking in loyalty than before;
  paid memberships (streaming, gaming, credit cards) command the strongest loyalty.
- The benefits consumers value **vary by region** (US / Europe / Asia-Pacific).

**Implication for AAA Insights:** the product's theme/sentiment analysis and conversational surveys
should help companies surface *what specific customers value and expect* — including personalization
and experience drivers — and segment those signals (e.g., by age cohort and region), which is exactly
where this research says the differentiation now lives.
