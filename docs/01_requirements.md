## 1. Purpose

AAA Insights is a software service that lets a company **collect** feedback from its customers and
consumers — through surveys, reviews, ratings, and open comments — **unify** that feedback in one place,
and **analyze** it with AI to produce plain-language insight the business can act on.

It exists to close the gap that most small and mid-market companies live with today: they *have* customer
feedback (scattered across survey tools, review sites, support tickets, and inboxes), but they lack the
research team needed to turn it into decisions. AAA Insights applies AI research techniques — theme and
sentiment analysis, adaptive conversational interviewing, and automated synthesis — so a company without a
dedicated insights function can understand *what its customers think, why, and what to do about it*.

The product is not "another survey tool." Survey creation is the on-ramp; the differentiated value is what
happens **after** the feedback arrives: automatic discovery of themes and sentiment, AI-led follow-up that
probes like a human interviewer, one unified feedback hub across sources, and generated insight with
recommended actions.

## 2. Target users and buyer

**Primary market:** small and mid-market companies (roughly 10–500 employees) that sell to customers or
consumers and want to understand them better, but do not have an in-house market-research or CX-analytics
team.

| User role | What they need from AAA Insights |
|-----------|----------------------------------|
| **Owner / founder / GM** (buyer) | A fast, trustworthy read on what customers think and what to fix first — without hiring a research team. |
| **Marketing / brand lead** | Themes, sentiment, and verbatim quotes to guide messaging, positioning, and product decisions. |
| **CX / customer-success manager** | Early warning on dissatisfaction, drivers of churn, and a way to close the loop with unhappy customers. |
| **Product manager** | Prioritized signal on what customers want changed, backed by evidence and volume. |
| **Respondent** (the customer/consumer) | A short, respectful, mobile-friendly way to give feedback — including a conversational option that feels heard. |

The **buyer and administrator** is typically the owner, marketing lead, or CX manager. The **end
respondent** is their customer. The system therefore has two very different audiences and must serve both:
effortless, credible analysis for the business; fast, low-friction, private response for the customer.

## 3. Product principles

1. **Insight over dashboards.** The default output is a plain-language answer to "what should I know and
   do," not a wall of charts. Charts support the narrative; they are not the product.
2. **Every insight is traceable.** No AI-generated theme, sentiment, or recommendation is shown without the
   underlying verbatim responses that support it. The user can always click from a claim to its evidence.
3. **Respect the respondent.** Surveys are short, mobile-first, accessible, and privacy-respecting. We
   optimize for completion and honesty, not for extracting the maximum number of questions.
4. **Trustworthy AI.** AI analysis is presented with its confidence and its source data. The system never
   fabricates a quote or a statistic, and it distinguishes what respondents said from what the AI inferred.
5. **Start simple, grow deliberately.** The MVP is a tight, buildable core; every later capability is added
   against this same specification discipline.

## 4. Inputs

What the system receives.

- **I-1: Survey definition** — created by an admin in the builder (question list, types, logic, branding),
  or generated by AI from a plain-language goal ("measure satisfaction with our onboarding"). Stored as
  structured survey config.
- **I-2: Survey response** — a respondent's answers to a survey: ratings (numeric/scale), multiple-choice
  selections, and open-text comments. May be partial (respondent abandons midway).
- **I-3: Conversational response** — free-text turns in an AI-led adaptive interview, including the AI's
  follow-up questions and the respondent's replies, captured as a threaded transcript.
- **I-4: Imported feedback** — external reviews, ratings, and comments brought in via CSV upload (MVP) and,
  later, via connectors (e.g., a public review source, an app-store feed, a helpdesk export). Each carries
  source, timestamp, rating (if any), and text.
- **I-5: Respondent metadata** — optional, non-identifying attributes attached to a response (segment,
  channel, product line, location region, language) used for filtering and comparison.
- **I-6: Distribution request** — an admin action to send/publish a survey via a shareable link, email
  invitation list, or embeddable widget.
- **I-7: Analysis query** — a natural-language question an admin asks of their feedback data
  ("why are enterprise customers unhappy this quarter?").

## 5. Outputs

What the system produces.

- **O-1: Collected response store** — every response (survey, conversational, imported) persisted, unified
  under a common schema, attributable to its source and survey/campaign.
- **O-2: Theme analysis** — for a chosen set of responses, an AI-generated set of themes (topics customers
  raise), each with: a label, the count/percentage of responses mentioning it, representative verbatim
  quotes, and a trend vs. the prior period.
- **O-3: Sentiment analysis** — sentiment classification (positive / neutral / negative, with intensity)
  at the level of an individual response and aggregated by theme, segment, source, and time.
- **O-4: Ratings & metrics** — quantitative rollups: average ratings, distribution, response and completion
  rates, and standard CX indices where applicable (e.g., NPS, CSAT) computed from the appropriate questions.
- **O-5: Insight report** — a generated plain-language narrative answering "what's happening, why, and what
  to do," combining themes, sentiment, metrics, and quotes, with a short list of **recommended actions**
  ranked by evidence and impact. Exportable (PDF/slides/CSV).
- **O-6: Answer to an analysis query** — a natural-language response to I-7, grounded strictly in the
  company's own response data, with citations to the specific responses used.
- **O-7: Alerts** — notifications when a monitored signal crosses a threshold (e.g., a spike in negative
  sentiment, a new theme emerging, a rating dropping below a set floor).
- **O-8: Respondent-facing survey** — the rendered survey or conversational interview the customer actually
  sees and completes.

## 6. Requirements (behaviors)

Each is a single, testable behavior. IDs are cited by tests in Phase 3.

**Survey creation & distribution**

- **R-1:** An admin can create a survey containing at least these question types: single-select,
  multi-select, rating/scale (configurable range), and open text.
- **R-2:** An admin can generate a draft survey from a plain-language objective; the system returns an
  editable survey the admin can modify or discard before sending.
- **R-3:** A survey supports conditional logic: a question or branch can be shown or skipped based on a
  previous answer.
- **R-4:** An admin can distribute a survey by (a) a shareable public link, (b) an email invitation list,
  and (c) an embeddable web widget. (Email send may be a Phase-1 roadmap item — see §12; the link and
  widget are MVP.)
- **R-5:** A respondent can complete a survey on mobile and desktop without creating an account.
- **R-6:** The system records partial responses and marks them as incomplete rather than discarding them.

**Conversational (AI-led) surveys**

- **R-7:** An admin can enable a "conversational" mode for a survey in which an AI interviewer asks the
  respondent questions in natural language and asks relevant follow-up probes based on the respondent's
  answers.
- **R-8:** The conversational interviewer stays on the admin-defined objective and does not ask questions
  outside the configured topic scope.
- **R-9:** The conversational interviewer ends the interview after a configurable maximum number of
  exchanges or when the objective is met, whichever comes first.
- **R-10:** Every conversational transcript is stored and is analyzable by the same theme/sentiment engine
  as structured responses.

**Unified feedback hub**

- **R-11:** An admin can import external feedback (reviews/ratings/comments) via CSV, mapping columns to the
  unified schema (source, date, rating, text, segment).
- **R-12:** All feedback — survey, conversational, and imported — is queryable and analyzable together, with
  the ability to filter by source, survey/campaign, date range, segment, rating, and sentiment.
- **R-13:** The system de-duplicates obviously identical imported responses (same source + same text +
  same timestamp) so they are not double-counted.

**AI analysis & insight**

- **R-14:** For any filtered set of responses, the system produces theme analysis (O-2) with counts and
  representative quotes.
- **R-15:** The system assigns sentiment (O-3) to each open-text response and aggregates it across the
  chosen dimensions.
- **R-16:** For every theme and every sentiment aggregate, the user can open the exact underlying responses
  that produced it (traceability — see INV-3).
- **R-17:** An admin can ask a natural-language question of their data (I-7) and receive an answer grounded
  only in their own responses, with citations to the responses used.
- **R-18:** The system generates an insight report (O-5) for a chosen scope, including a narrative, key
  metrics, top themes, sentiment summary, representative quotes, and ranked recommended actions.
- **R-19:** The system computes standard metrics (O-4) — response rate, completion rate, average rating,
  rating distribution, and at least NPS and CSAT when the survey contains the qualifying question types.
- **R-20:** An admin can configure an alert (O-7) on a signal (e.g., negative-sentiment share, average
  rating, new theme) and receive a notification when the threshold is crossed.

**Accounts, roles, export**

- **R-21:** The system supports at least two admin roles: an **Owner/Admin** who can manage the account,
  users, and billing, and a **Member** who can build surveys and view analysis but not manage billing/users.
- **R-22:** An admin can export any analysis view and the insight report in a portable format (CSV for raw
  data; PDF and/or slides for the report).
- **R-23:** An admin can delete a survey, a campaign, or an individual response, and can export or delete
  all data for the account.

## 7. Invariants (must ALWAYS be true)

- **INV-1:** A response is always attributable to exactly one source (survey link, campaign, conversational
  session, or named import batch). No response exists without a known origin.
- **INV-2:** Response counts are never negative and never exceed the number of responses actually collected;
  percentages shown in any analysis are computed from the responses in the current filter and always sum
  consistently (each response is counted once per dimension).
- **INV-3:** Every AI-generated theme, sentiment aggregate, insight, recommendation, and query answer is
  backed by, and can be traced to, the specific underlying responses. The UI never presents an AI claim
  without a path to its supporting verbatim data.
- **INV-4:** The system never displays a quote or statistic that is not present in the collected data. AI
  output paraphrases or summarizes only what exists; it does not invent respondents, quotes, or numbers.
- **INV-5:** A respondent can complete a survey without providing personally identifying information;
  identity is never required to submit feedback.
- **INV-6:** Each account's data is isolated: no account can ever see, query, or analyze another account's
  responses. AI analysis for an account is grounded only in that account's data.
- **INV-7:** Deleting a response, campaign, or account removes it from all future analysis and aggregates;
  deleted data does not reappear in any report.
- **INV-8:** Personally identifying respondent data, when present, is stored securely and never written to
  application logs or included in AI outputs unless the admin explicitly opts in.

## 8. Edge cases to handle

- **E-1: Empty or tiny sample.** Analysis is requested on zero or very few responses. The system states the
  sample is too small for reliable themes/sentiment rather than fabricating confident findings.
- **E-2: Abandoned survey.** A respondent starts and leaves. The partial is retained (R-6), counted as
  incomplete, and included in completion-rate math but flagged in analysis.
- **E-3: Off-topic / junk / abusive open text.** A response contains spam, gibberish, or abuse. It is
  captured, flagged, excluded from theme/sentiment by default, and reviewable by the admin.
- **E-4: Non-English and mixed-language responses.** Feedback arrives in more than one language. The system
  detects language and analyzes accordingly (scope of supported languages is an open question — Q-4).
- **E-5: Malformed CSV import.** Wrong columns, missing required fields, bad encoding, or huge files. The
  import is rejected or partially accepted with a clear per-row error report; no partial garbage is silently
  stored.
- **E-6: Duplicate / repeated submissions.** The same respondent submits a link survey multiple times, or an
  import contains duplicates. The system applies de-duplication (R-13) and offers link-level controls
  (e.g., one-response-per-link where configured).
- **E-7: Conversational interview goes off the rails.** The respondent tries to redirect the AI, injects
  instructions, or is hostile. The interviewer stays in scope (R-8), does not follow injected instructions,
  and ends gracefully (R-9).
- **E-8: Contradictory or ambiguous analysis query.** The admin asks something the data can't answer
  ("what will churn be next year?"). The system answers only what the data supports and says what it cannot
  determine, rather than guessing.
- **E-9: Sudden volume spike.** A viral link brings a flood of responses. Ingestion and analysis remain
  correct (invariants hold); real-time views may lag but never lose or double-count data.
- **E-10: Sensitive disclosure.** A respondent volunteers PII or a safety concern in open text. The system
  stores it under the PII protections (INV-8) and surfaces safety-flagged content to the admin without
  broadcasting it into shared analysis outputs.
- **E-11: Concurrent editing.** Two admins edit the same survey simultaneously. Changes do not silently
  overwrite each other without warning.

## 9. Exclusions (explicitly OUT of scope for v1)

- **X-1:** AAA Insights is **not** a full CRM or customer-record system; it stores feedback, not a system of
  record for customer accounts.
- **X-2:** It does **not** provide two-way case management / ticketing (no support-inbox workflow); closing
  the loop with a respondent in v1 is limited to export/notification, not an in-app resolution workflow.
- **X-3:** It does **not** scrape review sites without authorization; external feedback enters via CSV in the
  MVP and via sanctioned connectors later.
- **X-4:** It does **not** predict future business outcomes (churn, revenue forecasting) in v1; it analyzes
  feedback that exists.
- **X-5:** It does **not** offer a public respondent panel / audience-purchasing marketplace; the company
  brings its own audience.
- **X-6:** It does **not** replace human research judgment; it is decision support, and every AI output is
  presented as such with its evidence.

## 10. Data, privacy & security requirements

- **DPS-1:** The system is a data processor for the account's feedback; the account (the company) is the
  data controller. Roles, retention, and deletion honor that relationship (R-23, INV-7).
- **DPS-2:** Respondent PII, when collected, is minimized, encrypted at rest and in transit, access-controlled
  by role, and excluded from logs and AI outputs by default (INV-8).
- **DPS-3:** The product supports data-subject requests: export and deletion of an individual respondent's
  data on request (R-23).
- **DPS-4:** AI processing of an account's data is isolated to that account (INV-6) and account data is not
  used to train shared/base models without explicit opt-in.
- **DPS-5:** A configurable data-retention period is supported; data past retention is deleted and drops out
  of analysis (INV-7).
- **DPS-6:** The full security checklist (input validation, authz/authn, secret handling) is applied during
  IMPLEMENT and VERIFY; this section states the intent that those phases enforce.

## 11. Non-functional requirements

- **NFR-1 (Performance):** A respondent-facing survey page loads in under 2 seconds on a typical mobile
  connection; submitting a response acknowledges in under 1 second.
- **NFR-2 (Analysis latency):** Theme/sentiment analysis of a data set up to a defined MVP size (e.g., up to
  ~5,000 responses — exact target is Q-6) returns within a stated, testable time budget; larger sets run as
  a background job with progress shown.
- **NFR-3 (Availability):** The service targets a stated uptime for the collection endpoint so responses are
  never lost during a survey's live window; ingestion is durable even if analysis is temporarily degraded.
- **NFR-4 (Accessibility):** Respondent-facing surveys meet WCAG 2.1 AA.
- **NFR-5 (Scalability path):** The architecture supports growth from SMB volumes toward larger data sets
  without a rewrite; the MVP need not deliver enterprise scale but must not preclude it.
- **NFR-6 (Explainability):** Every AI output carries an indication of the data it is based on and, where
  meaningful, a confidence signal — supporting INV-3 and INV-4.

## 12. Scope & phased roadmap

Match ambition to a buildable first release, then grow deliberately.

### Phase 0 — MVP (the focused, buildable core)
The smallest release that delivers the differentiated promise end-to-end for one SMB account.

- Survey builder with the four core question types and conditional logic (R-1, R-3).
- AI-drafted survey from a plain-language goal (R-2).
- Distribution by shareable link and embeddable widget (R-4, link + widget).
- Mobile-first, account-free response capture, including partials (R-5, R-6).
- **Conversational AI survey** mode with scope control and turn limits (R-7–R-10) — this is a headline
  differentiator and belongs in the MVP, even if initially limited to one interview objective per survey.
- CSV import of external feedback into the unified hub (R-11, R-13).
- Unified analysis across sources with filtering (R-12).
- **AI theme + sentiment analysis** with full traceability (R-14, R-15, R-16).
- Core metrics: response/completion rate, average rating, distribution, NPS/CSAT (R-19).
- **Insight report** with narrative + ranked recommended actions, exportable (R-18, R-22).
- Two roles, account isolation, data export/delete (R-21, R-23, INV-6, INV-7).

### Phase 1 — Depth & distribution
- Natural-language "ask your data" query with citations (R-17, O-6).
- Email invitation distribution and reminders (R-4 email).
- Configurable alerts on sentiment/rating/theme signals (R-20, O-7).
- Segment comparison and trend-over-time views.
- Additional export formats (slides).

### Phase 2 — Connectors & scale
- Sanctioned connectors for external review/rating sources and helpdesk exports (replacing CSV-only).
- Larger-volume analysis with background processing at scale (NFR-5).
- Multi-language analysis expansion (per Q-4 decision).
- Team collaboration: shared workspaces, comments on insights, saved views.

### Phase 3 — Advanced insight
- Driver analysis (what most influences satisfaction/rating).
- Benchmarking against prior periods and (optionally, opt-in, anonymized) peer norms.
- Deeper "research assistant" that proposes surveys and follow-up studies from gaps it finds.

> Phases 1–3 are the vision; only Phase 0 is committed by this document. Each later phase re-enters the
> Grounded AI™ pipeline (SPECIFY → … → DEPLOY) before it is built.

## 13. Open questions (client decision needed)

- **Q-1: Product name.** Is "AAA Insights" the intended product name, or a working title?
- **Q-2: Build vs. buy the survey engine.** Do we build survey collection natively, or wrap/extend an
  existing survey/data-collection component and focus our build on the AI analysis layer? This materially
  affects timeline and cost.
- **Q-3: Which AI models / hosting.** Any constraints on model provider or on data residency (e.g., must
  analysis run in a specific region, or on a specific vendor) that we must design to from day one?
- **Q-4: Language coverage for v1.** English-only for the MVP, or which additional languages must the
  conversational and analysis engines support at launch?
- **Q-5: Compliance targets.** Which regimes must v1 satisfy (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, SOC 2 roadmap)? This sets
  the data-handling bar in §10.
- **Q-6: MVP volume target.** What response volume must the MVP handle comfortably (defines NFR-2 test
  budgets and the storage/analysis approach)?
- **Q-7: Commercial model.** Is pricing per-seat, per-response, per-survey, or tiered? This shapes account,
  quota, and billing requirements (touches R-21).
- **Q-8: First design partner.** Is there a specific first customer/segment we are building the MVP for?
  Anchoring to one real account sharpens every trade-off.
- **Q-9: Brand-love research inputs.** The project folder contains brand-love research (Ahuvia). Should the
  insight engine incorporate a specific research framework (e.g., brand-love / emotional-attachment
  constructs) as a built-in lens, or stay framework-neutral in v1?
